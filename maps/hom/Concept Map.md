# HOM Concept Map

Two maps covering all concepts. Starred concepts (⭐) are the most essential.

- [[Concept Map — Structure]] — Ch. 1–9: Production, Manager's Work, Decisions & Planning, Organization, Modes of Control
- [[Concept Map — People]] — Ch. 10–16: Motivation, TRM, Performance, Compensation, Training, Hiring

* Key cross-group patterns visible in the map:
	- Leverage is the central hub — connects to every other major cluster
	- TRM is the bridge between Motivation & Control and People Management, receiving edges from Maslow, Modes of Control, One-on-One, and Delegation
	- Indicators and MBO form a loop (planning generates objectives → objectives generate indicators → indicators inform next plan)
	- Training and Performance Appraisal both feed back into Managers Output

## Concept Outliner

### Production · Ch. 1–2

- [[Limiting Step ⭐]] — the slowest stage in any process; constrains [[Production Operations]], [[Production Tradeoffs]], and [[Lowest Value Stage]]
- [[Production Operations]] — the three types of production work (process, assembly, test); feeds into [[Quality Assurance]]
- [[Lowest Value Stage]] — reject defects here, before more value is added; connects to [[Quality Assurance]] and [[Limiting Step ⭐]]
- [[Production Tradeoffs]] — the tension between delivery time, output, and inventory; shaped by the [[Limiting Step ⭐]]
- [[Quality Assurance]] — inspection strategy tied to where in the process defects are cheapest to catch; enables [[Delegation ⭐]]
- [[Build to Forecast]] — production decision driven by lead times when demand uncertainty is high; tracked via [[Indicators ⭐]]
- [[Work Simplification]] — systematically reducing steps in a process; increases [[Leverage ⭐]] and supports [[Indicators ⭐]]
- [[Indicators ⭐]] — counters and measures that make process health visible; loops back to [[Management by Objectives ⭐]] and drives [[Leverage ⭐]]

### Manager's Work · Ch. 3–4

- [[Managers Output ⭐]] — a manager's output is the output of his organization; achieved through [[Leverage ⭐]], [[Delegation ⭐]], and [[Managerial Activities]]
- [[Leverage ⭐]] — the multiplier on a manager's time; central hub connecting [[Delegation ⭐]], [[One-on-One ⭐]], [[Management by Objectives ⭐]], [[Performance Appraisal ⭐]], and [[Training ⭐]]
- [[Delegation ⭐]] — transferring tasks to subordinates to free up high-leverage time; requires [[Quality Assurance]] checkpoints and is governed by [[Task Relevant Maturity ⭐]]
- [[One-on-One ⭐]] — the primary vehicle for information exchange and coaching; calibrated by [[Task Relevant Maturity ⭐]] and a form of [[Leverage ⭐]]
- [[Managerial Activities]] — the modes of work available to a manager (information, decisions, nudging); shaped by [[Leverage ⭐]] and [[Managers Output ⭐]]
- [[Meeting Types]] — the taxonomy of meetings (one-on-ones, staff, operation reviews, ad hoc); subdivides into [[One-on-One ⭐]], [[Staff Meeting]], and [[Operation Review]]
- [[Staff Meeting]] — structured forum for peers to share information and align; a type of [[Meeting Types]]
- [[Operation Review]] — cross-functional presentation of work for outside scrutiny; a type of [[Meeting Types]] and a source of [[Leverage ⭐]]

### Decisions & Planning · Ch. 5–6

- [[Decision Making Process]] — the three-stage model (free discussion → clear decision → full support); links to [[Knowledge vs Position Power]], [[Peer Group Syndrome]], and [[Six Decision Questions]]
- [[Knowledge vs Position Power]] — the divergence between who has expertise and who has authority; shapes how [[Decision Making Process]] works
- [[Peer Group Syndrome]] — peers avoid deciding because no one feels authorized; a dysfunction in [[Decision Making Process]] fixed by peer-plus-one
- [[Six Decision Questions]] — who decides, who consults, who ratifies, who informs; operationalizes [[Decision Making Process]]
- [[Planning Process]] — close the gap between environmental demand and present capability; produces [[Management by Objectives ⭐]] and draws on [[Leverage ⭐]]
- [[Management by Objectives ⭐]] — objective + key results, nested through the org; produced by [[Planning Process]], tracked via [[Indicators ⭐]], and a form of [[Leverage ⭐]] and [[Delegation ⭐]]

### Organization · Ch. 7–9

- [[Hybrid Organization ⭐]] — Grove's Law: mature organizations mix mission-oriented and functional units; relates to [[Centralization vs Decentralization]] and [[Team of Teams]]
- [[Dual Reporting]] — two supervisors for two dimensions (operational and functional); a structural feature of [[Hybrid Organization ⭐]] and [[Two-Plane Organization]]
- [[Centralization vs Decentralization]] — scale favors centralization; local knowledge and motivation favor decentralization; the tension that produces [[Hybrid Organization ⭐]]
- [[Two-Plane Organization]] — the operating plane plus coordinating planes (peer groups, task forces); a structural pattern within [[Dual Reporting]]
- [[Team of Teams]] — as organizations scale, management shifts to coordinating a network of teams; the scaling context for [[Hybrid Organization ⭐]] and [[Centralization vs Decentralization]]

### Motivation & Control · Ch. 10–11

- [[Modes of Control ⭐]] — free market, contractual obligation, or cultural values; which mode applies depends on CUA and the individual's self-interest alignment; bridges [[Hybrid Organization ⭐]], [[Delegation ⭐]], [[Managers Output ⭐]], and [[Task Relevant Maturity ⭐]]
- [[Maslow's Hierarchy]] — the needs hierarchy that explains what motivates at each level; drives [[Competitive Environment]], informs [[Task Relevant Maturity ⭐]], and underpins [[Compensation as Feedback]]
- [[Competitive Environment]] — creating racetracks (clear measures + competition) to activate self-actualization; an output of [[Maslow's Hierarchy]]

### People Management · Ch. 12–16

- [[Task Relevant Maturity ⭐]] — capability on this specific task determines the effective management style; receives edges from [[Maslow's Hierarchy]], [[Modes of Control ⭐]], [[One-on-One ⭐]], and [[Delegation ⭐]]; feeds [[Performance Appraisal ⭐]] and [[Training ⭐]]
- [[Performance Appraisal ⭐]] — the highest-leverage feedback tool; improves skill and motivation; connected to [[Task Relevant Maturity ⭐]], [[Leverage ⭐]], [[One-on-One ⭐]], [[Managers Output ⭐]], and [[Conflict Resolution Stages]]
- [[Conflict Resolution Stages]] — ignore → deny → blame → assume responsibility → solve; the emotional sequence within [[Performance Appraisal ⭐]] when confronting a serious problem
- [[Training ⭐]] — manager-led instruction tied to actual practice; one of the highest-[[Leverage ⭐]] activities and feeds back into [[Managers Output ⭐]] and [[Performance Appraisal ⭐]]
- [[Compensation as Feedback]] — bonuses, salary, and promotion as task-relevant signals; linked to [[Maslow's Hierarchy]], [[Performance Appraisal ⭐]], and [[Peter Principle]]
- [[Peter Principle]] — achievers alternate between meets/exceeds requirements until they settle; recycling as the correct response to over-promotion; a pattern within [[Compensation as Feedback]]
- [[Interviewing]] — structured process to predict future performance; highest-risk form of [[Performance Appraisal ⭐]]-style judgment with the least available information
- [[Handling Resignation]] — immediate, undivided response when a valued employee quits; failure to act confirms the employee's sense of being unvalued
