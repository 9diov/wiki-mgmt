# Teacher Pay Analogy

In many school systems, an excellent teacher and a poor teacher earn identical salaries if they have equal tenure. Performance is not evaluated in any way tied to compensation.

## What Grove Observes

Grove uses this as an illustration of what happens when compensation is fully decoupled from performance. He goes a step further: he wonders whether the pass/fail grading system—in which teachers make no distinction between levels of student achievement—originated in how teachers themselves are paid. When management gives no incentive to distinguish levels of performance, that indifference becomes the norm at every level of the system.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Compensation as Feedback]]**: When salary is tied purely to experience rather than output, the signal that performance matters disappears. The consequence is not neutral—it actively discourages differentiation and performance evaluation throughout the organization. The teacher pay system is an extreme version of experience-only salary administration, and Grove uses it to show how that model's message ("performance doesn't matter") propagates into behavior.
