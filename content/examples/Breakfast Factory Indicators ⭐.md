# Breakfast Factory: Five Daily Indicators

As manager of a scaled breakfast factory, what five things would you check first every morning?

## The Five Indicators

1. **Sales forecast** — How many breakfasts should be delivered today? Paired with yesterday's variance (planned vs. actual) to calibrate confidence in the forecast.

2. **Raw material inventory** — Enough eggs, bread, and coffee on hand? Too little: order more now. Too much: cancel today's delivery.

3. **Equipment condition** — Did anything break down yesterday? Rearrange the production line if needed before the day begins.

4. **Manpower** — Are all staff present? If two waiters called in sick, decide now: call in temporary help or reassign from another station.

5. **Quality indicator** — A customer complaint log maintained by the cashier. If a waiter drew unusual complaints yesterday, address it before the shift starts.

## What It Illustrates

These five indicators give the manager a complete picture of the operation's health *before* problems become crises. The complaint log pairs with throughput: high volume means nothing if customers are being treated poorly. This demonstrates **paired indicators**—every quantity metric needs a quality counterpart to prevent gaming and overreaction.

The principle applies equally to any administrative operation: define the five (or so) indicators you would check every morning, and you've defined what you actually manage.
