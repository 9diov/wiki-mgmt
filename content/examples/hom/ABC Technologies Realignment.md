# ABC Technologies Realignment

A lightly fictionalized press release (names changed) that Grove reproduces from a trade newspaper. Three-year-old ABC Technologies reorganizes as its product line broadens.

## The Reorganization

ABC Technologies creates three mission-oriented product divisions:
- Super System Division
- Ultra System Division
- Hyper System Division

Each division head is promoted from a functional role (engineering director, sales/marketing VP, product design manager) and now owns full product marketing and product development responsibility for their division.

Meanwhile, two major functional organizations remain centralized at the corporate level, serving all three divisions:
- Sales (newly named VP)
- Manufacturing (newly named VP)

All division heads and the two functional VPs report to the CEO.

## Why It Happened

As the product line broadened, the company needed units that could stay close to each product area and respond quickly. But sales and manufacturing retain enough scale advantages—and enough cross-unit interdependence—to justify keeping them centralized.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Hybrid Organization ⭐]]** in action, and **Grove's Law**: as a company grows, it inevitably adds mission-oriented units while retaining functional groups for shared services. The pattern is not unique to Intel or large enterprises—a three-year-old startup is already doing it.
