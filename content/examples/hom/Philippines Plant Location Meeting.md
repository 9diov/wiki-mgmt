# Philippines Plant Location Meeting

A mission-oriented meeting called to decide the specific location for a Philippine plant expansion. Four attendees: Far East Plant Manager, Manufacturing Manager, Corporate Construction Manager, and the President. Held via teleconference connecting Santa Clara and Phoenix.

## The Agenda

| Time | Item | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 11:00–11:30 | Manufacturing considerations | F.E. Plant Manager |
| 11:30–12:00 | Construction considerations | F.E. Construction Manager |
| 12:00–12:45 | Review of alternatives, including preferred choice | F.E. Construction Manager |
| 12:45–1:00 | Discussion | All |

The agenda was sent in advance, named each attendee, stated the meeting's purpose explicitly, and assigned a presenter to each block.

## What It Illustrates

A well-run **[[Meeting Types|mission-oriented meeting]]**: the chairman did the necessary work beforehand. The objective was clear (decide a location, not discuss it). Roles were assigned. Time was structured. The meeting could produce a decision because everyone arrived knowing what they were there to do.

Contrast with the common failure mode: a chairman who shows up "as just another attendee" hoping things will develop as desired. When mission-oriented meetings fail, the blame belongs to the chairman.
