# U.S. Embassy London: Visa Processing

The American Embassy in London processes approximately 1 million visa applications per year, employing 60 staff who handle up to 6,000 applications per day. At any time, 60,000–80,000 British passports are in the embassy's hands. Lines of 100 or more applicants form outside the building daily.

## The Attempted Solutions
The embassy tried newspaper ads asking tourists to apply early, a three-week turnaround commitment, and drop-off boxes for same-day service. None resolved the backlog—these only added logistical complexity without increasing throughput.

## The Production Analysis
98% of applicants are approved without question. The embassy is performing a 100% gate inspection when a sampling monitor would suffice.

**The fix**: Institute a sampling inspection, as the IRS does with tax returns. Scrutinize a selected sample according to predetermined criteria; pass the rest. The IRS achieves compliance across millions of filers without auditing every return—the same principle applies here.

This would break the logjam without materially increasing the risk of undesirable visitors entering the country.

## What It Illustrates
Three principles at once:
1. **Work simplification**: The embassy never questioned whether processing 100% of applicants was actually necessary. It wasn't.
2. **Monitoring vs. gate inspection**: When most material passes, a gate inspection is wasteful. Variable sampling achieves the same quality goal at a fraction of the cost and disruption.
3. **Output vs. activity**: The embassy's expediting schemes increased activity (more classification categories, more logistics) without increasing output (applications processed).
