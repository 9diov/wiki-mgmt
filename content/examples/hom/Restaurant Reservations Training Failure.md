# Restaurant Reservations Training Failure

Grove and his wife made a dinner reservation at a restaurant. The woman who took the call mentioned she was new and didn't know all the ropes. No specific issue was communicated.

## What Happened

When the Groves arrived, they learned the restaurant had lost its liquor license—guests needed to bring their own wine. The maitre d' asked if they had been told this on the phone. They had not.

Throughout the evening, the maitre d' went through the same apologetic routine with every party he seated. Nobody had wine. The new employee, presumably never instructed to inform callers of the situation, had created a broken experience for every customer that night.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Training ⭐]]**: One untrained employee, in a simple customer-facing role, created a systemic failure that repeated with every customer. The cost was not just inconvenience—it was the maitre d's time, every guest's experience, and the restaurant's reputation. The cost of proper training would have been negligible; the cost of skipping it compounded with every reservation.
