# Cindy: The Know-How Manager

Cindy is an engineer at Intel who supervises an engineering group in a wafer fabrication plant. She also sits on an advisory body that establishes standard technical procedures used across all Intel fabrication plants.

## Two Roles, Two Types of Output

**As a supervising engineer**: Her activities increase the output of her own plant directly—she manages her team, solves local problems, and improves local processes.

**As an advisory body member**: She disseminates specialized technical knowledge to all other Intel wafer fab plants. Her recommendations become the standard procedure everyone follows.

## The Leverage of the Second Role
In the advisory role, Cindy influences the output of every neighboring organization that uses the standardized process. She has no authority over those plants—she doesn't manage them—yet her work shapes how they operate.

## What It Illustrates

**Manager's output includes neighboring organizations.** A manager's (or specialist's) output isn't limited to their direct reports. Influence through expertise, coordination, and knowledge-sharing counts.

**Know-how managers are middle managers.** Cindy doesn't need a large org chart to exert managerial-scale influence. Her unique technical knowledge makes her a de facto manager of the process she defines, regardless of her title.
