# Venture Capitalist Second Million

A venture capitalist has already made ten million dollars. They are still working hard to make another ten.

## The Puzzle

Physiological, safety, and social needs clearly don't apply—ten million dollars exceeds any material requirement many times over. The venture capitalist doesn't publicize their successes, so esteem and recognition from others aren't the driver either.

## The Explanation

At the level of self-actualization, money is no longer a *means* to material ends—it is a *measure of achievement*. The second ten million is as motivating as the first, not because of what it buys, but because of what achieving it *means*. The need for achievement has no ceiling: reaching a goal doesn't extinguish the drive, it calibrates the next target.

## The Test

A simple diagnostic: if the *absolute size* of a raise matters most, the person is in physiological or security mode. If what matters is *how the raise compares to what others received*, they are in esteem or self-actualization mode—money has become a scorecard, not a grocery list.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Maslow's Hierarchy]]**: money as motivator transitions in character as one moves up the need hierarchy. Understanding which mode a person is in changes how a manager should interpret their relationship to compensation and recognition.
