# Breakfast Factory National Franchise

After one Breakfast Factory becomes a success, it franchises to 100+ locations nationwide. Each franchise is run by a local entrepreneur who knows their neighborhood; together, the network has enormous purchasing power and a brand reputation worth protecting.

## The Centralize/Decentralize Decisions

| Function | Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Equipment purchasing | Centralized (Chicago) | Vendor expertise and incoming inspection capability took years to build; duplicating per branch is wasteful |
| Quality standards | Centralized | Any branch's failure damages the whole brand |
| Local advertising | Decentralized | Local manager knows which publications their community reads |
| Wage scales | Decentralized | Labor markets vary significantly by region |
| Core menu | Centralized | Customers expect consistency across locations |
| Regional menu items | Local discretion | Regional culinary tastes differ |
| Egg purchasing | Regional centers | Must be fresh; national shipping is impractical, but per-branch inspection duplicates effort |
| Tableware | National purchase, regional warehouses | Uniform brand identity; but requisitioning Chicago for broken plates is impractical |
| Franchise location selection | Central with regional input | Chicago lacks local market knowledge; regional managers provide it |
| Real estate standards | Standards centralized, execution local | Ensure brand consistency while using locally available buildings |

## What It Illustrates

The **[[Centralization vs Decentralization]]** analysis applied comprehensively: there is no single right answer across all functions. Equipment centralizes; wages decentralize; eggs use a regional hybrid. Each function requires its own evaluation of whether scale/consistency benefits outweigh the value of local knowledge.

Also demonstrates **[[Team of Teams]]**: the franchise network requires organizational structures—regional centers, corporate overhead, national standards bodies—that simply didn't exist when it was one restaurant.
