# Meeting Cost Calculation

Grove estimates a manager's loaded cost (salary + overhead) at approximately $100 per hour.

## The Math

A meeting with 10 managers lasting 2 hours costs the company **$2,000** in managerial time.

Most expenditures of $2,000 require advance approval—buying a copier, authorizing a transatlantic trip. Yet any manager can convene a meeting and commit the equivalent sum with no approval process at all.

## The Implication

Before calling a meeting, ask: *What am I trying to accomplish? Is a meeting necessary? Desirable? Justifiable?* If all three answers aren't yes, don't call it.

Even as an *invited* participant, you should ask whether your attendance is justified. If you conclude it isn't, tell the chairman—and do it early, at a low-value-added stage, before others have prepared materials around your presence.

The same logic applies to lateness. Allowing people to arrive late without consequence is permitting them to steal $100/hour from every person in the room.

## What It Illustrates

Meetings are a resource allocation decision, not a social formality. Treating them as such—with the same discipline applied to capital expenditures—is a direct application of [[Leverage ⭐]] thinking: is this the best use of this many hours of this many managers?
