# Intel Philippines MBO

Grove uses the Philippine plant expansion decision (also discussed in Chapter 5 as an example of the Six Decision Questions) as an MBO case study, showing how the same decision was expressed as a formal objective with concrete, dated key results.

## The MBO Structure

**Far East construction manager's objective**: "Obtain decision on Philippine plant expansion"

**Key results**:
1. Complete study of land availability (near existing plant and at other acceptable locations) — by June
2. Complete financial analysis showing trade-offs between land costs, construction costs, and operating costs for both locations
3. Present results to the plant location steering group and obtain their decision
4. Have Grove ratify the decision — by October

All four key results were accomplished; the objective was met.

## The Nesting

The construction manager's supervisor had an objective: "Ensure all plant expansion projects stay on schedule." One of *his* key results read: "Obtain Philippine plant expansion decision by October"—the same wording as his subordinate's objective. A clean example of the nesting hierarchy: the subordinate's objective is the supervisor's key result.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Management by Objectives ⭐]]** applied to a concrete business decision: specific, dated key results with no ambiguity at deadline; objectives that nest across levels; and the importance of making key results unambiguous so the person holding them knows without question whether they have been completed.
