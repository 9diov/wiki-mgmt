# Caribbean Assembly Plant Workers

Intel operates an assembly plant in the Caribbean where the standard of living is low. Intel wages provide a substantially higher standard than most of the local population enjoys.

## What Happened

In the early years of operation, many employees worked just long enough to accumulate a specific sum of money—then quit. Having reached their own predetermined threshold for "enough," more money and a stable job offered no additional motivation.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Maslow's Hierarchy]]** at the physiological level: money motivates powerfully when it is buying basic necessities, but *only up to the point where those needs are met*. Once a person has what they needed money for, the motivational force of additional money drops to near zero.

This is not unique to any culture or context—it is the fundamental property of all needs below self-actualization: they self-extinguish when satisfied. The only level that doesn't is self-actualization, where more achievement feeds the drive for further achievement rather than satisfying it.
