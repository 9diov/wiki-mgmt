# Intel College Recruiting

Intel needs to hire college graduates. Managers visit campuses, interview seniors, invite promising candidates to the plant, and extend offers.

## The Problem
The plant visit is expensive: Intel pays for travel, and multiple managers spend significant time interviewing each candidate. If many candidates visit but few receive offers, the expensive step is being used wastefully.

## Applying Production Principles

**[[Limiting Step ⭐]]**: The plant visit—most expensive in time and money—is the limiting step. The whole flow should be designed to protect and maximize its yield.

**Solution**: Add phone screens before issuing plant visit invitations. Screens filter out weaker candidates, raising the ratio of offers-extended to visits-made. The limiting step is used only on candidates who have already passed a cheaper gate.

**Time offsets**: Working backward from graduation (the delivery date), the recruiter staggers all steps—on-campus interviews, phone screens, plant visits—so each happens at the right point in the months preceding June.

**[[Lowest Value Stage]]**: Screening out a weak candidate during a 30-minute phone call (low cost) is far better than discovering the same mismatch after flying them in for a full day of interviews.
