# Robin: Annual Planning Process

Robin is an Intel finance manager responsible for setting up the company's annual financial planning process.

## The Situation
Before the planning cycle begins, Robin defines exactly what information needs to be gathered at each stage, what format it should take, and who is responsible for what. She lays this out in advance of the process starting.

## The Leverage
This upfront work directly affects the subsequent work of approximately **200 managers** who participate in planning. By eliminating ambiguity before it occurs, Robin prevents confusion that would otherwise ripple through the entire organization over weeks.

## Why Timing Matters
The same work performed *after* the planning cycle has started—scrambling to help individual managers define their own guidelines—has far less leverage. The problem is no longer systemic; each fix is local. The total effort required is higher and the impact is lower.

## What It Illustrates
**High-leverage activity through scale**: one person affecting many. But critically, the leverage depends on *when* the work is done. Work done in advance of a process shapes the entire process. Work done reactively patches individual instances.

This also demonstrates that leverage isn't inherent to an activity type—it depends on timing and context.
