# Sales Training Program

A company needs to train its sales force to sell a new product.

## The Three Operations

**Process**: Raw product data (specs, competitive pricing, market research) is converted into coherent selling strategies that salespeople can actually use. Raw material (data) is transformed into something more useful (strategy).

**Assembly**: The selling strategies, market data, pricing information, brochures, handouts, and flip charts are combined into a single unified presentation. Components are joined into a new whole.

**Test**: A "dry run" presentation is delivered to a selected group of field sales personnel and managers. If it fails—objections aren't addressed, messaging is unclear—the material goes back for rework before being deployed to the full sales force.

## What It Illustrates
Any knowledge-work process has the same three phases as manufacturing. Recognizing them reveals where quality can fail and where rework should be inserted. The dry run is an in-process test: it catches problems before the "product" (a trained sales force) is deployed—at a much lower cost than retraining after a failed product launch.
