# John Wayne Manager

A middle manager, fresh from a top business school, grew frustrated with Intel's group decision-making process. He quit and joined a company that promised during the interview that individuals were free to make decisions and implement them without committee process.

## What Happened

Four months later, he returned to Intel. His explanation: if he could make decisions without consulting anybody, so could everybody else.

## What It Illustrates

The interdependence at the heart of the **[[Decision Making Process]]**: unilateral decision authority is only attractive if others don't also have it. In a knowledge business, the "onerous" process of group discussion exists precisely because other people's decisions affect your work, and vice versa. The desire to skip it is understandable; the consequences of skipping it reveal why it's unavoidable.
