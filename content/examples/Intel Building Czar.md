# Intel Building Czar

For years, Intel's facilities maintenance group—responsible for keeping buildings clean and neat—performed mediocrely. Pressure and inducements produced no lasting improvement.

## The Intervention

A program was introduced in which each building's upkeep was periodically scored by a resident senior manager designated as a "building czar." The scores were then compared publicly across all buildings.

## What Happened

The condition of all buildings improved dramatically, almost immediately. Nothing else changed: employees received no additional money, no other rewards. The only thing introduced was a score and a comparison—a racetrack.

## What It Illustrates

The central example of **[[Competitive Environment]]** in practice: objective measurement combined with peer comparison creates the motivational structure of competitive sport. People who had no particular stake in cleaning buildings suddenly had one: being lowest-ranked was visible and avoidable.

Also demonstrates that meaningful **[[Indicators ⭐|indicators]]** don't need to be complex—a single score publicly compared is enough to change behavior when the work is real and the comparison is honest.
