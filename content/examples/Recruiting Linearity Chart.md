# College Recruiting Linearity Chart

Intel plots cumulative offer acceptances against the months of the recruiting season, comparing actual progress to the ideal straight-line path toward the semester's hiring target.

## The Situation
If the target is to hire X graduates by June, the ideal is a steady pace—a straight line from zero to X across the months. By April, the chart shows actual acceptances well below the line.

## What It Reveals
To hit the target, the team would need acceptances to arrive at a far higher rate over the remaining two months than they managed in the preceding four. This is likely impossible. The linearity chart makes this visible in April, not in June.

Without the indicator, the shortfall is discovered only after graduation season ends—too late to act.

## What It Illustrates
**Leading indicators** give advance warning so corrective action is still possible. A linearity indicator is a specific type: it tracks whether progress is on pace or whether you're banking on an unrealistic acceleration. The value is not just knowing you're behind—it's knowing early enough to do something about it.

The same indicator applies to any activity with a linear target: monthly production output, sales quotas, software milestones.
