# Intel Sales One-on-One

During a one-on-one, Grove's subordinate—the head of Intel's sales organization—walked him through trend indicators of incoming orders. Grove was vaguely familiar with the data but hadn't studied it closely.

## What Happened

The sales manager laid out specific figures and argued that Intel's business had stopped growing—and that the summer slowdown was not just seasonal. Together, Grove and the sales manager examined the data alongside other industry indicators and reached a shared conclusion: business was genuinely decelerating.

This meant taking a conservative approach to near-term investment—a significant decision.

## The Cascade

Grove left the meeting and shared the conclusion with all the business groups he supervised. The sales manager scaled back growth plans in his own area. A single one-on-one propagated a major strategic posture across the company.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Leverage ⭐]] of the one-on-one**: 90 minutes of meeting time produced a high-quality decision that changed the plans of multiple groups. The mechanism was information-sharing building a *common base*—both parties left with the same understanding and made aligned decisions independently, without Grove having to instruct each group individually.

This is also an example of the subordinate teaching the supervisor—the information flow running upward, not just downward.
