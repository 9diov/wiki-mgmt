# Intel Employee Orientation Lecture

Grove is the first lecturer in Intel's employee orientation program—a series in which senior managers present to all incoming professional employees. He covers the company's objectives, history, management systems, and values.

## Two Roles at Once

**Information-giving**: Grove delivers detailed content about Intel to a group of ~200 new employees at their most receptive moment. One session, two hundred people, each of whom will carry what they learned into years of work.

**Role modeling**: Grove's *conduct during the session*—how he handles questions, whether he listens carefully, how he handles a challenge—demonstrates Intel's values more vividly than any slide. New employees aren't just hearing about how Intel operates; they're watching it.

## The Bonus: Information-Gathering
The questions new employees ask reveal their concerns and their understanding level—signal that Grove wouldn't have access to through any other channel. A large population, recently hired, asking unfiltered questions is a high-quality information source.

## What It Illustrates
A single activity can have multiple types of leverage simultaneously. The orientation lecture is high-leverage because:
- **Scale**: one person affecting 200 at once
- **Timing**: people are most impressionable when new
- **Dual function**: information-giving and role modeling in the same session
