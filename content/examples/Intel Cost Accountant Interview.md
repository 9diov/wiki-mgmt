# Intel Cost Accountant Interview

Grove needed to fill a cost accountant position at Intel. The candidate was a Harvard MBA from the food service industry—he knew nothing about the semiconductor business, and Grove knew nothing about finance. Standard technical questioning was impossible for both sides.

## What Grove Did

He walked the candidate through the semiconductor manufacturing process step by step, answering any specific questions the candidate had. Then he asked: "What would the finished cost of a wafer be?"

The candidate paused, asked clarifying questions, and then began reasoning through semiconductor cost accounting from first principles—discovering some of the principles as he went. He arrived at the correct answer.

He was hired.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Interviewing]]**: When direct technical assessment is impossible, a hypothetical situation can reveal problem-solving capacity more accurately than any résumé or credential. The candidate's answer mattered less than *how* he got there. The exercise correctly predicted that his problem-solving capacity was first-rate—as his subsequent performance confirmed.
