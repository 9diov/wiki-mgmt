# Intel Division Sales Force Loyalty

One of Intel's divisions ran into serious problems and was left with no product to sell for nearly a year. The sales engineers—who could easily have found other jobs and immediate commissions elsewhere—stayed.

## What Happened

Despite having nothing to sell, and despite the immediate financial cost of foregone commissions at competing firms, the Intel sales engineers by and large remained with the company. They believed Intel would recover and had faith that things would eventually improve.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Modes of Control ⭐|Cultural values]]** overriding self-interest under adversity: belief and faith are not aspects of market-mode behavior. They stem from shared experience, a sense of common purpose, and trust in the organization—exactly the cultural mode of control.

This is the same sales force that, in the [[Intel Sales Commission Contest]] example, responded to market-mode incentives by favoring high-commission products. The contrast shows that the mode of control people are governed by is not fixed—it depends on how the situation is framed and what has been invested in building the underlying culture.
