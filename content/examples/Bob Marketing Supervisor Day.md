# Bob Marketing Supervisor Day

Bob is an Intel marketing supervisor. Grove traces his day to show how all three modes of control govern the same person's behavior within a single workday.

## Three Modes in One Day

**Buying lunch in the cafeteria** → *Free-market forces*
Bob's choices are defined and based on what he wants to eat and what he's willing to pay. He is purely serving his own preferences; no one oversees the transaction.

**Coming to work** → *Contractual obligations*
Bob is paid a salary in exchange for showing up and doing his best. The employment relationship is the contract; it implies he must be present even when he'd rather not be.

**Participating in strategic planning activities** → *Cultural values*
Strategic planning is outside Bob's regular job as defined by his contract. It demands extra effort with no immediate personal reward. He does it because he believes the company needs what he can contribute—group interest overriding self-interest.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Modes of Control ⭐]]** coexist within a single day for a single person. The goal isn't to choose one mode for a person or a company—it's to recognize which mode is governing each type of interaction and act accordingly.
