# Intel Career Development Course

Intel brought in outside consultants to teach a career development course for employees.

## What Happened

The consultants used a highly structured, academic approach: multi-year career plans, carefully coordinated job rotations, formal sequencing. This was entirely at odds with Intel's actual practice, which operated more like a free market—employees were informed of internal openings and expected to apply on their own initiative.

Participants noticed the gap between what was being taught and how things actually worked at Intel. Rather than gaining useful guidance, they became demoralized by the contradiction.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Training ⭐]]**: Training delivered by outside specialists, no matter how well-designed in the abstract, fails when it teaches practices incompatible with the organization's actual way of operating. The credibility problem is twofold: the instructor is not a practicing authority on *this* organization's methods, and the content actively conflicts with what participants observe every day. Employees need training that reflects reality—not an idealized external model.
