# Intel Pricing Engineer

An Intel marketing engineer is responsible for setting prices for the company's product line.

## The Situation
Hundreds of salespeople in the field depend on these prices to close deals. If prices are set too high, salespeople cannot win business regardless of how hard they work. If prices are set too low, Intel gives away margin.

One person, one set of decisions, affects the daily effectiveness of an entire salesforce.

## What It Illustrates
**High leverage through unique knowledge.** The pricing engineer isn't managing anyone. They have no direct reports. But their specialized knowledge—understanding of costs, competitive positioning, market dynamics—gives them enormous influence over the output of the entire field organization.

The same pattern appears in:
- A development engineer with unique knowledge of a manufacturing process (controls how it's used across all product designers)
- A geologist in an oil company
- An actuary in an insurance firm

These "knowledge specialists" or "know-how managers" possess leverage that can exceed that of many traditional managers with large teams.
