# Rickover Chair Trick

Admiral Rickover, who personally interviewed every candidate for the Navy's nuclear submarine program, reportedly seated candidates on a deliberately three-legged chair. When it tipped over, the candidate was left sprawling on the floor. Rickover believed this tested strength of character under embarrassment.

## What It Illustrates

**[[Interviewing]]**: Grove cites this as a cautionary contrast to his own approach. Tricks and stunts create false first impressions of the work environment. A candidate who is hired will carry those first impressions for a long time before they are corrected. The interview should show the manager and the company as they actually are—so that any match is based on reality, not theater.
