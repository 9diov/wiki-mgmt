# Standard Work

Standard work is the documented definition of the current best method for performing each production task. It is not a permanent rule but a baseline — the foundation from which improvement is measured and the mechanism by which improvement is sustained.

## The Three Elements

Every standard work sheet defines:

**1. Cycle time**
The time allotted to complete one unit. Derived from required output: cycle time = available operating time ÷ required daily quantity. This is takt time in practice — the heartbeat of the production system that each process must match. If a worker cannot complete the task within cycle time, the process has a problem that must be investigated.

**2. Work sequence**
The order of operations the worker performs: what they pick up first, how they mount the part, when they move to the next machine. This is not the flow of materials through the process — it is the specific motion sequence of the individual operator. Differences in sequence cause differences in time; standardizing sequence reduces variability.

**3. Standard inventory**
The minimum work-in-process within the process needed to keep operations flowing. In a process organized by flow (machine sequence), standard inventory is typically just the items currently mounted on machines. In a process organized by function, more WIP is required — and that extra WIP is itself an indicator of an improvement opportunity.

## Who Writes It

Ohno is explicit: standard work must be written by the production person doing the work, not by an engineer at a desk. A procedure written from observation of reality — and revised through trial — is usable. A procedure written from theory is not.

*"A proper work procedure cannot be written from a desk. It must be tried and revised many times in the production plant. Furthermore, it must be a procedure that anybody can understand on sight."*

This means supervisors and foremen must be capable of writing standard work. It is a core management skill in TPS, not a specialist function.

## Standard Work as Visual Control

Standard work sheets are posted prominently at each workstation. This serves two functions:
1. The operator has a constant reference and cannot claim ignorance of the method
2. Any supervisor walking the floor can immediately see whether the work being performed matches the standard

Deviation from standard is the definition of an abnormality. The andon system and autonomation surface machine abnormalities; posted standard work surfaces human-process abnormalities.

## Standard Work Is Not a Ceiling

The most important property of standard work: it is the current best method, not the permanent method. **Improvement is the process of changing the standard** — finding a better sequence, reducing cycle time, eliminating a step entirely. Without a documented standard, there is no baseline from which improvement can be measured, and there is no way to sustain an improvement once found.

Ohno: *"All that we are doing is looking at the time line, from the moment the customer gives us an order to the point when we collect the cash. And we are reducing the time line."* Standard work is how each reduction is locked in.
