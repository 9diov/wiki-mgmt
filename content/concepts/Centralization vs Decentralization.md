# Centralization vs Decentralization

The fundamental organizational tradeoff in a multi-unit enterprise: centralize for scale advantages and consistency; decentralize for local knowledge and adaptability. The right position on this spectrum differs by function—there is no single policy that applies everywhere.

## The Case for Centralization
- Purchasing power and vendor expertise scale with volume (specialized equipment, uniform tableware)
- Quality consistency protects brand reputation across all units
- Expensive specialized capabilities (incoming inspection, real estate acquisition) are wasteful to duplicate per unit

## The Case for Decentralization
- Local knowledge cannot be fully transmitted to headquarters: labor markets, neighborhood tastes, local media
- Local managers have stronger motivation and faster reaction time than central staff
- Central mandates applied uniformly to heterogeneous local conditions produce suboptimal results

## Hybrid Solutions
When neither extreme works, intermediate structures emerge:
- Regional purchasing centers (not national, not per-unit) for fresh goods requiring nearby sourcing
- National purchase combined with regional distribution warehouses for branded items
- Centrally set standards with locally executed decisions

## The Key Principle
Each organizational function must be evaluated independently on whether scale/consistency benefits outweigh lost local flexibility, or vice versa. The answers come out differently across functions in the same business—and the right balance shifts as the business evolves.
