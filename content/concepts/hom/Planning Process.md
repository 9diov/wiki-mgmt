# Planning Process

A manager's core planning task: compare where the environment is headed against where current activities will take you, then act today to shape tomorrow's outcome.

## The Three Steps

**Step 1 — Environmental Demand**
Examine what customers, competitors, technology, and your operating environment will require of you in the future—not just now. Focus on the *difference* between current demand and expected future demand; only new actions are needed to address that difference.

**Step 2 — Present Status**
Determine what your organization will actually produce if nothing changes—accounting for current projects, their completion rates, and realistic attrition. Use the same "currency" as your demand estimate so the two can be directly compared.

**Step 3 — Close the Gap**
Identify what new actions are needed to make projected output match projected demand. This gap defines your *strategy*—the most abstract summary of actions still meaningful to you. What implements the strategy is tactics. Note: a strategy at one managerial level is a tactical concern at the next higher level.

## The Core Insight
Today's gap is a failure of past planning. You cannot fix it today. Planning means identifying actions you must take *now* to avoid problems that will surface later—like stopping for gas before the tank is empty, not after.

The true output of planning is not a document but the decisions made and actions taken as a result of the thinking process. Intel's annual plan is valuable not as a bound volume but as the organizational behavior it changes.

## Planning Frequency
Plan often enough to get useful feedback on previous decisions, but not so often that you have no time to observe their effects. At Intel, the annual planning cycle drives decisions for the *next year only*—subsequent years are replanned in the following cycle.

## Who Plans
Planning must be done by operating managers, not a separate planning staff. The leverage of planning—enormous, because it shapes future output—can only be realized when the people who make the plan are the same people who execute it.
