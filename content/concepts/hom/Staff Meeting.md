# Staff Meeting

A process-oriented meeting involving a supervisor and all of their direct subordinates simultaneously. Its distinctive value is peer interaction—something neither one-on-ones nor operation reviews provide.

## Why Peer Interaction Matters

A supervisor hearing two subordinates with opposing views debate an issue learns far more than from either one alone. The friction of peer disagreement produces insight. Staff meetings are also the training ground for group decision-making—a capability that matters for dual reporting structures and cross-functional bodies.

## What Belongs on the Agenda

Items that affect more than two people present. If a discussion narrows to two people working on something that doesn't concern anyone else, the supervisor should redirect and have those two continue offline.

Structure: mostly controlled with an advance agenda (to allow preparation), plus an open session for housekeeping and emerging issues that don't yet warrant scheduled time.

## The Supervisor's Role

Moderator and facilitator—not lecturer. The supervisor's job is to keep discussion on track and ensure the subordinates do the substantive work. Using staff meetings to pontificate is the surest way to kill free discussion.

The staff meeting resembles a family dinner: the group knows each other well, informal authority is established, and everyone knows who tends to overclaim, who needs prompting, and who has real expertise in what area. This familiarity makes it an effective decision-making venue.
