# Operation Review

A formal presentation meeting that brings together managers who don't otherwise interact—typically junior presenters, their supervisors, and senior reviewers several organizational levels above them.

## Purpose

Cross-level teaching and learning. Junior managers get feedback and suggestions from senior managers who see the bigger picture. Senior managers get ground-level detail from people who live inside the problems. Both leave better informed than they would be from their normal interactions alone.

A secondary purpose: motivation. Presenters know they are being seen by their supervisor's supervisor and by peers from other parts of the organization. This raises preparation quality.

## Four Roles

**Organizing supervisor**: the presenting managers' direct supervisor. Helps shape what gets covered and at what level of detail, handles logistics, and paces the presentations during the meeting.

**Reviewing manager**: the senior supervisor the review is aimed at. Should *not* preview the materials in advance—spontaneous reaction is what makes their engagement valuable. Their role is to ask questions, make comments, and set the tone of openness. As a role model for junior managers in the room, they must take this seriously.

**Presenters**: use visual aids; allow ~4 minutes of presentation and discussion per slide. Watch the audience for comprehension and engagement signals; adjust pace accordingly.

**Audience**: has an active obligation. Ask questions, flag errors, speak up on disagreements. Attendance is paid work, not a spectator event.
