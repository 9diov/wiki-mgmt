# Management by Objectives

A system that makes planning concrete and operational for short time horizons: define where you want to go (objective) and how you will know you are on track (key results). Designed for quarterly or monthly cycles where environmental demands are relatively well understood.

## The Two Questions
1. **Where do I want to go?** → The *objective*
2. **How will I pace myself to see if I'm getting there?** → The *key results*

Key results must be specific and dated. At deadline, there should be no ambiguity: either you completed it on time, or you didn't.

## Nesting Hierarchy
Objectives nest across levels. A subordinate's objective, when met, should automatically advance their supervisor's objective. The supervisor's objective then becomes the subordinate's tactical assignment. This alignment ensures that individual goal-completion accumulates into organizational goal-completion.

## The Focus Requirement
Limit objectives to a small number. A long list of objectives means no objectives—attention diffuses and nothing is truly prioritized. Choosing few objectives is also the act of explicitly saying "no" to everything excluded.

## MBO Is a Pacing Tool, Not a Contract
The system is a feedback mechanism for the person doing the work—a stopwatch they hold themselves. It is *not* a legal document for performance review. A person who meets all their key results but fails their stated objective may still have performed well (Columbus found the New World instead of a trade route, yet served Isabella's goals beyond all expectation). Mechanically rating someone on objective completion alone misuses the system.

## Examples
- **[[Columbus MBO]]** — Isabella's objective → Columbus's objective → Columbus's key results; Columbus missed his stated objective, met his key results, and delivered incalculable value; illustrates nesting hierarchy and why MBO is a pacing tool
- **[[Intel Philippines MBO]]** — the Far East construction manager's objective and four dated key results for the Philippine plant expansion; his objective was a key result for his supervisor, demonstrating perfect nesting
