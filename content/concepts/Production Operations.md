# Production Operations

Every production process—physical or otherwise—consists of three fundamental operation types.

## The Three Types

**Process**
Physically or chemically transforms material. Changes the nature of what you're working with.
- Boiling an egg
- Converting raw product data into sales strategies
- Writing individual software modules from specifications

**Assembly**
Combines components into a new, unified whole.
- Plating a breakfast (egg + toast + coffee)
- Combining sales strategies, pricing data, and handouts into a presentation
- Linking software modules into a complete compiler

**Test**
Examines components or the completed product against required standards. Failed material is sent back for rework or scrapped.
- Visual check that coffee is steaming and toast is browned
- "Dry run" sales presentation with field personnel
- Unit tests and system tests in software development

## Why This Matters
Recognizing these three types in any workflow—manufacturing, recruiting, training, administrative—lets you apply production thinking universally: where are the process steps, where is assembly happening, and where are the tests? This reveals where defects can enter and where to place inspection points.
