# Delegation

Assigning tasks to subordinates as a means of multiplying a manager's effective output. Done well, it is high leverage. Done poorly, it produces worse outcomes than not delegating at all.

## The Core Requirement
Delegation only works if the delegator and delegatee share a **common information base** and common approach to solving problems. Without this shared foundation, the delegatee can only act with constant specific instructions—which is just meddling in disguise, producing low leverage.

## Delegation ≠ Abdication
You remain responsible for the outcome of any task you delegate. The delegation transfers the work, not the accountability. Monitoring the delegated task is the only way to ensure results.

> Delegation without follow-through is abdication.

## What to Delegate
When you have a choice, delegate tasks you know **well**—these are easier to monitor intelligently. Resist the temptation to hold on to familiar tasks simply because you enjoy them. Be conscious of which tasks you're keeping and why.

## How to Monitor Without Meddling
Apply quality assurance principles to delegated work:
- **Review at the lowest-value stage**: check rough drafts, not polished finals. Don't let a subordinate invest days of effort before you discover a fundamental problem.
- **Variable frequency**: adjust how often you check based on the subordinate's *task-relevant maturity*—their experience and demonstrated performance on *this specific task*, not their general ability. Reduce monitoring as they prove reliable; increase it for new or unfamiliar tasks.
- **Sample, don't inspect everything**: checking all details of a delegated task is quality-assurance testing 100% of output—wasteful and corrosive to subordinate ownership.

## Monitoring Decision-Making
For tasks that involve judgment calls, monitor the *process* rather than every decision. Ask specific questions during a review; if the subordinate's reasoning is sound, approve the direction. This gives you confidence without requiring you to do the thinking yourself.
