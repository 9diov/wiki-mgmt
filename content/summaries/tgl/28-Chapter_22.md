# Chapter 22

## What Happens

One week in. The team reviews results: 12 overdue orders shipped — the 12 most overdue. The worst outstanding order has dropped from 58 days late to 44. The red/green tag system is working; bottleneck-bound parts are moving through the plant on an express lane. New late orders are still appearing, but fewer. Progress, not enough.

**QC results:** Pre-bottleneck inspection found 5% of parts entering the NCX-10 and 7% entering heat-treat were defective. Those parts are now caught before the bottleneck rather than wasting bottleneck time. Effective gain in throughput capacity with no added cost.

**Break coverage:** Staggered breaks at the bottlenecks are in effect, but reports come in that the NCX-10 is still occasionally sitting idle outside of break time. Donovan is investigating.

**Yellow tape — "treat like gold":** A new signaling layer is added for parts that have already passed through a bottleneck. After exiting the bottleneck, a strip of yellow tape is added to the tag. Plant-wide communication — posters, foreman announcements, newsletter — spreads the message: yellow-tagged parts represent time that has already been spent at the constraint and cannot be recovered. Handle them with extra care, prioritize their quality at every downstream step. Formal process improvement for bottleneck-routed parts is also underway.

**The Zmegma:** Bob Donovan disappears in the morning and reappears at the loading dock with a flat-bed truck. Under a canvas tarp: an ancient machine called a Zmegma — obtained free from the South End plant (written off as scrap, cost only the truck rental). Combined with two other idle old machines already in the plant, the Zmegma can perform the same operations as the NCX-10, albeit manually and with slightly higher error rates.

Jonah had asked whether any of the older machines the NCX-10 replaced were still around. The answer had been no — they were sold to make room for the inventory holding pen. Bob found a workaround: an equivalent set sourced from a sister plant. The NCX-10 bottleneck now has backup capacity at essentially zero capital cost. The chapter ends as they plug in the Zmegma and it whirrs to life.

## Note

No new concepts introduced. The chapter shows Jonah's two exploitation levers in motion: eliminating waste at the bottleneck (QC placement, break coverage, yellow tape) and offloading work to non-bottlenecks (the Zmegma). The progress is real but insufficient — the team knows it needs to go faster.
