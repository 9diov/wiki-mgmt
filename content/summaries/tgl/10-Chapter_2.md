# Chapter 2

## What Happens

Rogo slips home at 6:30 PM to find Julie in a new outfit, expecting the dinner out he'd promised the night before. He can't stay — the NCX-10 is damaged and the rush order still isn't shipped. Their exchange goes badly: when Rogo mentions the plant might close, Julie's first question is where they'd move next, not whether he can save it. He returns to the plant without dinner.

Back on the floor, Donovan has mobilized the entire workforce on a single order — people hand-carrying parts one at a time across departments, everything else idle. It's wildly inefficient by any metric, but Order 41427 ships at 11 PM. Rogo and Donovan celebrate at Sednikk's Bar and Grill.

Over burgers and beer, Rogo tells Donovan they can't run the plant this way every day — the economies of scale would collapse. Donovan concedes: "Maybe I learned too many of the wrong things back when I was an expeditor." Rogo admits he'd probably do the same thing next time.

Alone in his office afterward, Rogo thinks through the situation clearly for the first time. The plant has good technology, decent people, and a real market. Yet nothing ships on time, inventory piles up, and the plant loses money. He can't identify what's wrong. He blames the Japanese. Then the cost cuts he's already made. Then his own blind spots. He ends the chapter exhausted and no closer to an answer.

## The Problem Deepens

- **The fix is unsustainable.** Getting 41427 out required stripping resources from everything else. The plant won by breaking every rule it normally runs by.
- **Efficiency and performance are diverging.** The plant runs high efficiency numbers yet loses money and ships late. Rogo senses the contradiction but can't resolve it.
- **The personal toll is accumulating.** Julie's detachment from the plant's fate ("Did you ask about your next job?") signals that the marriage is fraying alongside the business.

## Management Observations

- **Local heroics don't equal systemic health.** Donovan's all-hands push shipped one order but left the rest of the backlog untouched — and left the plant more fragile.
- **Expediting is a learned reflex, not a strategy.** Donovan's instinct to "just see what had to be done" is exactly what the novel will dismantle.
- **The right question isn't being asked yet.** Rogo's late-night monologue cycles through causes (competition, costs, people, technology) without ever questioning whether the plant's operating model itself is the problem.
