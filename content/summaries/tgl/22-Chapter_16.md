# Chapter 16

## What Happens

Rogo and Dave return from the hike Sunday afternoon. The house is half-empty — Julie's clothes are gone. Dave found a note on the kitchen table.

The note is short: *"I can't handle always being last in line for you. I need more of you and it's clear now that you won't change."* She's gone away to think things over. Sharon was dropped at Rogo's mother's house without explanation.

Rogo retrieves Sharon and brings the kids home. Sharon is terrified, convinced she did something wrong. He gives both children the standard reassurances. A silent pizza dinner. Nobody has anything to say.

He tries calling Julie's friend Jane — no answer. He tries Julie's parents — they don't know where she is. Her mother asks, *"What did you do to her?"* Her father suggests she was kidnapped. Rogo asks them to call him if they hear from her.

Past midnight, a car turns into the driveway. He leaps up. It's just someone doing a three-point turn. He goes back to staring at the ceiling.

## Note

No business concepts. The chapter delivers the domestic consequence of everything that's been building since Chapter 12. Julie's note echoes the hike directly — *"always being last in line"* — in language the reader now understands. The plant crisis and the marriage crisis have the same structure: Rogo's attention is a constrained resource, and he has been misallocating it. The chapter ends without resolution, stranded at midnight.
