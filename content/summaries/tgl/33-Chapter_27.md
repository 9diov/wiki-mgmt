# Chapter 27

## What Happens

**Division meeting.** Ethan Frost opens by stating that without Bearington's improvement, the UniWare Division would have reported losses for the seventh consecutive month. Bearington's surge is the only reason the division posted its first operating profit of the year.

**Results summary:**
- Overdue backlog: completely eliminated
- Throughput: dramatically up
- Inventory: falling steadily
- Shipment prediction: Ralph can predict ship dates to within ±1 day; marketing has a report and it's been accurate
- Efficiencies: dipped briefly when materials were withheld, then recovered as excess inventory was consumed and releases resumed

Peach's response: *"Good job, Al."* He remains non-committal. Hilton Smyth glowers. Peach wants 15% more improvement next month before he'll discuss removing the closure threat. Rogo says yes — then walks out and realizes he's promised too much.

**The new constraint.** The surge in performance was driven by clearing an enormous backlog of overdue orders. That backlog is now gone. The plant can ship faster than before, but it needs incoming orders to ship. With the production constraint removed, the system's limit has moved: it is now **the market**. Without more orders, there is no more throughput to gain.

> *"We've been eating up our backlog of orders at a terrific rate... Now that we've shipped all the overdues... where the hell am I going to get the orders that will give me an extra fifteen percent?"*

Rogo considers shipping July orders early in June — but recognizes this only creates a hole in July. He needs more business.

**Domestic.** Rogo detours to Forest Grove. Long walk with Julie by the river. She acknowledges they've been getting along well; she still fears going home because she believes the old patterns will return. Rogo applies his goal-framing instinct: *"What is the goal of a marriage?"* Julie resists the framing. Argument. Reconciliation. Saturday plans confirmed.

## The Constraint Has Moved

Chapter 27 is the pivot from production problem to market problem. The plant's original constraints — NCX-10, heat-treat, material release policy — have all been addressed. Throughput is up, inventory is down, the backlog is cleared. The system now has more production capacity than it has orders to fill.

This mirrors the broader TOC logic: every time you break a constraint, you find the next one. The previous constraint was internal (production). The next constraint is external (market demand). Managing this requires a different set of actions — not shop-floor scheduling, but sales, pricing, and competitive positioning.

## Management Observations

- **Clearing a backlog is not the same as growing the business.** Shipping overdue orders produces a one-time surge in throughput metrics. Sustaining the gain requires either new orders or further cost reduction — neither of which is available through the same methods that cleared the backlog.
- **Rogo withholds the full story from Peach.** He judges Peach is not ready to accept the reasoning behind the changes, and too early a disclosure would invite interference. This is a management judgment about sequencing, not deception — he plans to present the full case once the results are more durable.
- **The goal-seeking mindset transfers badly to personal relationships.** Rogo's instinct to ask "what is the goal?" is exactly right in manufacturing. Julie's reaction shows the same logic applied to a marriage lands very differently. The method doesn't fit the context.
