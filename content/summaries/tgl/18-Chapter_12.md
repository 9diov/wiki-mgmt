# Chapter 12

## What Happens

Rogo comes home from New York half-expecting the house to be empty. It isn't. Julie is at the kitchen table, red-eyed from a night out with a friend (and, it turns out, a singles bar — "it was Jane's idea").

They fight, then reconcile. Julie names the actual problem plainly: even when Rogo is home, he's somewhere else — the kids have to repeat themselves twice before he hears them. His career has come first for years, not just this crisis.

They make a deal: Rogo comes home for dinner most nights and does his paperwork at home afterward. "At least we could see you," she says. He agrees. They seal it.

Then he goes to the plant anyway.

## Note

No new business concepts. The chapter establishes the domestic stakes running parallel to the plant crisis — and signals that the same problem (being present vs. just being physically there) applies to Rogo's marriage as much as to his management. The weekend ahead is when the hike happens.
