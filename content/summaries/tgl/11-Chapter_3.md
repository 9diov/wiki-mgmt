# Chapter 3

## What Happens

Rogo drives to UniCo division HQ for an 8 AM meeting Peach has called with all his plant managers and staff. The agenda is secret, but the grapevine says it's about a bad first quarter and a new productivity drive.

In the parking garage, Rogo runs into Nathan Selwin, newly on Peach's staff. Selwin pulls him aside to share what nobody is officially saying: the entire UniWare division is going on the block. Granby (corporate CEO) gave Peach until end of year to improve performance — or the division gets sold. And Peach goes with it.

The implication hits Rogo immediately: the three-month clock isn't just about his plant. Peach is fighting for his own survival too, which explains the overreactions. It also means Rogo can no longer assume Peach would offer him a soft landing if the plant closes. He could be out of a job entirely.

Inside the meeting, Peach hands off to the division controller, who delivers the numbers: Q1 was catastrophic, the division is in real danger of a cash shortfall. Peach then delivers a mandate for productivity improvements across all plants. Rogo can't concentrate — fragments of management-speak wash over him as he sits there.

He reaches into his jacket for a pen and pulls out a cigar instead. He doesn't smoke. He stares at it: *And then I remember.*

## The Stakes Expand

Chapter 1 established a 3-month ultimatum for one plant. Chapter 3 reframes the whole situation: the division itself is at risk, Peach's career is on the line, and "improve productivity" is the only directive anyone has — with no clarity on what that actually means. The cigar is the bridge to Chapter 4, where the core intellectual problem of the novel is first posed.

## Management Observations

- **Crisis pressure distorts management behavior.** Rogo now understands why Peach has been acting erratically: he's operating from fear, not strategy. The insight doesn't solve anything, but it's the first sign of Rogo thinking systemically about people rather than just operations.
- **"Improve productivity" is not actionable without a definition.** Peach's meeting is full of numbers and targets — but as the next chapter will show, nobody in the room, including Rogo, can articulate what productivity actually means or whether they're currently measuring the right things.
- **The division's performance problem is structural, not local.** Every plant is struggling. This isn't one bad manager; it's a systemic failure of how the whole organization thinks about its work.
