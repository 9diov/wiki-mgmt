# Chapter 20

## What Happens

**Business:** The staff reconvenes after Jonah's visit. Lou has refined the bottleneck cost calculation overnight: only 80% of products flow through the bottlenecks, so the true cost is 80% of total operating expense divided by bottleneck hours — $2,188/hour, not $2,735. Still an eyeopener.

The team agrees on an action plan and moves immediately:

1. **QC before the bottlenecks** — Bob will have an inspector checking pre-bottleneck parts by end of day.
2. **Break rules** — Rogo will negotiate with the union to stagger breaks so the bottleneck never sits idle.
3. **Bottleneck priority by overdue orders** — Up to now, expediting was driven by whoever screamed loudest. New rule: the most overdue orders get first priority at the bottleneck. Ralph will generate a ranked list of all overdue orders by days late; Stacey will identify which missing parts still need to go through each bottleneck; Bob will schedule the bottlenecks accordingly. Everything not going through a bottleneck is assumed to be available or will be by the time the bottleneck parts arrive.

Rogo's summary to the team: *"We don't have time to take a step back and do some kind of headquarters number where everyone takes six months to think about it. We know what we have to do. Let's get it done."*

**Domestic:** That evening, Rogo drives to Forest Grove unannounced. Ada locks the screen door. He waits in the car for forty-five minutes. Julie finally comes out. They walk around the block in the dark.

She's not having an affair. She's been unhappy for a long time — and feels guilty about the unhappiness, as if she has no right to it. She's not ready to come home. Rogo tells her the choice: stay away and drift toward divorce, or come back and work on it. He can't make the decision for her. *"Give me a call when you know what you want."*

As he rolls down the window to leave: *"You know, I do happen to love you."* She comes to the car, reaches through the window, and kisses him. Then she turns and runs back into the house.

## Note

The business content is brief — implementation decisions following Jonah's visit, no new concepts introduced. The key management addition is the priority system: bottleneck scheduling driven by order due-date performance, not by expediting noise. The domestic arc reaches a provisional turning point: not resolved, but the ice has broken.
