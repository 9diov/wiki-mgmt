# Chapter 7: The Breakfast Factory Goes National

## Core Idea
Scaling a successful operation into a network creates a qualitatively different management challenge: how to capture economies of scale and protect quality consistency (which require centralization) while allowing local adaptation to market conditions (which requires decentralization). At this scale, management becomes a "team of teams."

## Key Concepts

**[[Centralization vs Decentralization]]**
Every organizational decision in a multi-unit enterprise sits somewhere on the spectrum between fully centralized and fully decentralized. The right position differs by function:
- *Centralize* when scale advantages dominate: equipment purchasing, quality standards, uniform tableware
- *Decentralize* when local knowledge dominates: wage scales, local advertising, some menu items
- *Hybrid* when neither extreme works: regional egg purchasing centers, national purchase with regional tableware warehouses

No single policy applies everywhere. Each function requires independent analysis of whether the benefits of coordination outweigh the cost of lost local flexibility.

**[[Team of Teams]]**
Running a single unit and running a network are structurally different problems. A network requires coordination infrastructure that didn't exist before—corporate personnel, traffic management, real estate acquisition staff—and demands decisions about what each local team should decide for itself versus what gets decided once for all. Management at scale is not a bigger version of managing a single team; it is fashioning a team of teams in mutually supportive relationship.

## Illustrative Examples

**[[Breakfast Factory National Franchise]]**: A hundred-plus locations create a decision matrix spanning equipment (centralize), wages (decentralize), eggs (regional centers), menu (hybrid), real estate (central standards, local execution). Demonstrates that [[Centralization vs Decentralization|centralization vs. decentralization]] must be resolved function by function—the same answer doesn't apply to everything.
